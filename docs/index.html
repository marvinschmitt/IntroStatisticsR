Introduction to Statistics with R

Page updated: 08.11.2021

Welcome to the course Introduction to Statistics with R (Einführung in die Statistik mit R)!

General

This website summarizes the material and general information of the course. The public material is provided in English, the teaching language is German. The assignments are available in the corresponding GitHub repository at https://github.com/marvinschmitt/IntroStatisticsR.

The participants will receive all non-public information (in-person meeting locations, online meeting login data, video material) via e-mail.

Course Outline

The course features five lessons R01 – R05, each with a focus topic covering the essentials of one fundamental statistical method. The sixth lesson R06 will give a brief introduction to handling complex and unhandy data (i.e., eye-tracking data). Before the first lesson, we will make sure that all software is well set-up (R00). After the last lesson, we will meet online for a conclusion and outlook on how to apply the statistical methods in your own research (R07).

Teaching mode

The course aims at using a suitable teaching format for each component. Therefore, we will have different modes of in-person + online as well as synchronous + asynchronous parts. The synchronous parts (i.e., meetings) take place on the following dates:

During each synchronous online lesson (R01-R05), we will focus on discussing a statistical method from a conceptual point of view. In addition, each lesson (R01-R05) will feature an asynchronous video that introduces the implementation of the corresponding method in R. Past experience has shown that asynchronous teaching of programming methods was perceived as superior to live teaching. That’s for one simple reason: Participants reported that they could simply pause or replay parts of the video in order to assure that they can follow the course. The link to the teaching videos will be provided to the participants via e-mail. The session on eye-tracking (R06) will be synchronous-only and give a brief introduction to handling more complex data from multiple source files.
